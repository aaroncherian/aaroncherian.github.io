Section 4

Tom looked to Lilah for support, but she pointedly ignored his gaze. He turned back to Enzo,

“Listen, Enzo. I just think you might need a little distance, just for a bit.” he said. “Shard contacted me a bit earlier, Headquarters wants her to head up a temporary team for a big job downtown with local police. She’ll need some heavy hitters, and I can recommend you for it. You need some time to grieve. We all do.”

Enzo started to protest, but Tom cut him off, a bit more forceful with his tone now.

“Do you think this is what Ayano would have wanted for us? Ignoring people in need to help track down her killer?”

“She would have wanted to be saving them herself!” Enzo’s fist hit the table, small blue sparks shooting outward as he connected.

There was a sharp cracking noise as the lightbulb hanging in the lampshade above them flickered and went out.

“Lorenzo!” Lilah said sharply.

Enzo sighed, and reached up to unscrew the bulb.

“Sorry,” he murmured. “I’ll get a replacement.” He trudged off in the direction of the bartender.
