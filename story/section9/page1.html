Section 9

When he opened them, he was alone in an empty bar. Even more snow had piled in now, and he heard the scrapes of glass being pushed along the floor by the wind. He sighed and stood up. Not feeling nearly as cold as he was earlier, he judged that he could leave his jacket behind. Idly, he thought about what he might do the next day. Maybe it was time to leave the city for a bit. Get a little distance.

He walked along the bar, past ruins and ashes to the door, and as he reached for the handle, he heard laughter.
