Section 9

When he opened them, he was alone in an empty bar. Tom sat there for a few minutes, staring down at the table as snow brushed past his face.

“I’m so sorry,” he whispered, before standing up.

Oddly, even though more snow had piled into the bar he didn’t feel as cold as earlier.

He looked out into the street.

And then Tom decided it was time to leave. Not just the bar, but the city itself. He would leave it behind, all of it.

He allowed himself a small smile. Starting a new life elsewhere. Yes, he could do that.

Tom walked along the bar, past ruins and ashes to the door, and as he reached for the handle he heard a voice behind him.
