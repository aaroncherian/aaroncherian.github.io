Section 8

There were a few moments of silence between them.

“Because she didn’t understand!” Tom shouted out finally. “She went around doing as she pleased and everyone else suffered for it! We had families to take care of, bills to pay!” He took a deep breath, and then continued in a calmer voice.

“How do you expect to have heroes to save a city when they can’t save themselves from being evicted from their own homes? We had heroes leaving, Lilah, because they had no choice. We were losing heroes, beginning to get understaffed on patrol teams. You didn’t see it, but I did, every captain did. We did what we needed to. It was the same for us as all of this was for you,” Tom said, indicating the inferno around them. “It wasn’t right, but it was necessary.

“Does it make you happy now, knowing why? Does it make all of this worth it?”

Lilah shook her head.

“No, I don’t feel better knowing at all, honestly. But I didn’t do this just for an explanation,” she said. “You will die today, Tom. But even if you don’t, I’ve done enough. My work is accomplished.”
