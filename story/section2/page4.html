Section 2

“You should be shutting down communication to Headquarters when you’re off-shift, its standard protocol now,” Tom reminded her, trying to keeping his voice calm.

Ayano shifted uncomfortably in her seat,

“A witness called in a mugging close by. It was simple job to do, and I knew I could get there before any other heroes could, and before anyone else got hurt. That’s all there is to it,” she said.

“Ayano, I’ve told you already. When your shift is done, it’s not your job to take any calls. Leave it to the other heroes on patrol,” Tom replied, taking in a deep breath, tremors shaking his voice slightly as anger slipped through.

“Actually, Tom I think it is my job to help someone that calls for help. I didn’t become a hero to ignore calls because my hours are up,” Ayano responded, clearly not budging on the topic.

The anger was harder to keep down now, and Tom felt it filling up his thoughts.

It just wasn’t as simple as helping people on a whim, not anymore. There were rules in place.

Tom clenched his fists, a response forming in his mind, saturated with his rising frustration and anger, but he felt a familiar presence pressing down on his mind.
