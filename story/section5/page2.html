Section 5

“The reunion never happened, did it?” He asked with growing horror.

Lilah’s head snapped up.

“How did you know?” She demanded.

“I thought there was a pattern. That I was just dozing off at our reunion and I would wake up between memories,” Tom responded, his voice quiet at first, but firmer as he spoke. “But then there was this, talking to you, someone that doesn’t exist in a place that doesn’t exist. Which means that either there is no pattern, or that I wasn't seeing the correct pattern. I've never really been waking up after all of these memories, have I? It's just been one continued hallucination.”

Lilah nodded rapidly. Her face lit up in a happy expression that was a stark contrast to the shock Tom was feeling.

“This is good, this is really good Tom. I’ll be able to use this next time. Even if you don’t remember, I will. We’ll be that much closer to breaking out,” she said.

Tom tried to respond but as he did he felt his muscles grow rigid.
