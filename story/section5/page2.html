“Don’t screw with me!” He snapped. “Tell me what’s going on!” Lilah looked back, unfazed.

“Ah, there’s the anger Tom. Why is it I never realized what an angry person you were?”

“What the hell does that mean?” Tom shouted, slamming his palms down on the table as he stood up. Lilah looked mildly startled,

“Well, I think if you take a look back on the past few seconds you’ll realize exactly what I mean,” she said, sighing. “This isn’t the type of thing that I can really tell you about, Tom. But, that doesn’t mean I can’t show you.”

She waved, and Tom felt the now all-too-familiar sensation of being tugged into a memory, the feeling of losing control over his body, and in some sense his mind. Bryan sat across next to Lilah now, all three of them in costume, but everything around them was still, frozen in time.
