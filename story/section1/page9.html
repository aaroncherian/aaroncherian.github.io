Section 1

“It’s surprising,” he said more solemnly. “There’s a lot about this place that hasn’t changed. I just thought more might have changed in the what, six years we’ve all been apart?”

“Seven,” Tom corrected him softly.

“The city still feels the same, oddly enough.” Lilah said.

“Not exactly. It hasn’t been the same since you guys left,” Tom said. “It just feels… empty, somedays. But that may be because I’m just a strange person.”

“What? You? Strange? Perish the thought!” Even between mouthfuls of food Enzo found the time to respond. Bryan brought both his hands up to his face, pantomiming a shocked expression.

Oh had Tom forgotten what it was like having the two of them in one room.

Lilah raised an eyebrow, and Enzo relented.

“But why do you think that, all of a sudden?” He asked.

“I guess, for thinking that a city with a quarter of a million people could ever feel empty,” Tom replied, chuckling at his own self-pity.

Lilah shook her head.

“No, it’s not Tom. Our entire team was close, you know, even before Ayano joined. And after she did, we became a unit. If you and Bryan were the brains, and Enzo and I the muscle, then she was the heart of it all,” Lilah said. “So it’s hard, not having the people you depended on around. We understand.”

We understand.

Just those two words made Tom unexpectedly happy to hear, lifting a certain weight off from him.
