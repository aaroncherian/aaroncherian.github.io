Section 1

“So you geniuses plan to dump water on the woman who controls it at will?” Lilah interjected.

Bryan and Enzo promptly released each other.

“Besides, the sprinkler system has been faulty for far too long. I keep telling them to replace it, but still nothing.” There was a touch of ire to Lilah’s voice.

“It’s a bar full individuals with powers that spend their days and nights patrolling the streets to fight other less-than-moral individuals with powers as a job,” Tom snorted. “You think the idea of a kitchen fire scares anyone here?”

“And that’s why they never get fixed,” Lilah retorted.

“Well you see, I also happen to know someone who’s exceedingly good at controlling fire,” Tom revealed, bringing his voice to a conspiratorial whisper. “Maybe even the best, so I feel there’s little to worry about.”

“Hmm, yes I find your flattery acceptable,” Lilah judged. Her eyes, dark as the wood of an oak tree, had a twinkle in them. She smiled and when she did, for the first time in recent memory, Tom felt whole, a dusty glass being filled after years of disuse.

It was good to be among friends.

Their food arrived shorly. Plates descended onto the center of the table, and Lilah went ahead and passed them to the appropriate person.

“Thank you Jaquelyn!” Tom called out to the bartender, who was surrounded by a swirling vortex of glasses and plates. His voice for the most part faded into the cacophony of chatter in the bar, but she glanced towards him and gave him a small salute.
